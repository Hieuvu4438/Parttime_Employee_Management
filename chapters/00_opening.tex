\chapter*{Opening}
In the context of restaurant chain operations, managing part-time employees presents significant administrative and operational challenges. Each restaurant operates on a shift-based system with fixed working hours, while employees are allowed to register their availability weekly under minimum working requirements. Ensuring sufficient staffing levels for every shift, maintaining fairness in shift allocation, and accurately calculating wages based on actual working hours require a systematic and well-structured management approach.
\\\\
This project, entitled “Part-time Employee Management System”, aims to analyze and design a software solution that supports weekly shift registration, scheduling, attendance tracking, wage calculation, and employee performance statistics. The system enforces priority rules in shift assignment, particularly favoring employees with fewer total scheduled hours when registrations exceed required staffing levels. Additionally, it integrates check-in and check-out mechanisms to ensure precise payroll computation, including overtime compensation and deductions for late arrivals or early departures.
\\\\
Through this project, theoretical knowledge in system analysis and design is applied to a practical workforce management scenario, contributing to improved efficiency, transparency, and consistency in part-time employee administration.
